Feel & performance — one goal

While you switch,
the rest of the world recedes.

ZenTab isn't really about its interface — it's about the focus it brings. Feel and performance are the same goal: a switch that stutters can't feel calm, and a switch that feels calm is, by definition, fast. So we judge every choice against both, and never brag about either.

Zero-friction, invisible

Switching never breaks your flow. The tool gets out of the way the instant you're done — and asks for nothing when you're not using it.

Single-tasking by default

The current task is the center of gravity. Each mode scopes tightly, so everything else feels put away rather than piled up.

Calm, not stimulating

A soft 80–120ms fade at the monitor's real refresh rate. Quiet, unhurried motion — no jarring transitions, no loud chrome.

Attention, in the moment

While you choose, the world behind dims and blurs. The choices step forward; everything else steps back until you've landed.

Calm by design

No settings.
On purpose.

Every setting is a small decision, and decisions pull you out of your flow — so ZenTab has none. We make the calls and keep you on one clear path. The calm comes from having nothing to fiddle with.

The one file that does exist is just for the keys that summon each mode. Open it once, then forget it's there.

~/.zentab.toml 
# great defaults out of the box —
# tune the keys to your hands.

[triggers]
everyday   = "cmd+tab"
current_app = "cmd+grave"
global     = "opt+tab"

[overlay]
theme = "dark"   # dark | light
